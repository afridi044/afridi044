---

**S.M Afridi Mahmud** — Final-year CSE student at **BUET**, focused on the hard problem of building AI systems that work outside the demo.

My work sits at the intersection of **language models**, **retrieval**, and **evaluation** — with a particular obsession: *if we can't measure what a system is doing, we don't understand it well enough.*

---

## Focus Areas

**LLMs for Code** — Refactoring, migration, transformation, and evaluation that goes beyond surface-level correctness.

**RAG Systems** — Hybrid retrieval, grounded generation, and knowledge-heavy workflows that stay reliable under real constraints.

**AI for Education** — Learning tools, assessment, and tutoring systems — especially in practical, low-resource settings.

**Measurement & Evaluation** — Reproducible scoring, diagnostic frameworks, and evaluation design that actually tells you something.

---

## Selected Work

#### `pinned-contract-eval` — *PHP Migration Evaluation Framework*
Policy-grounded evaluation for LLM-based code migration. Built around reproducible scoring, pinned upgrade contracts, and cross-oracle diagnostics. The core question: *how do we evaluate AI-generated code changes rigorously enough to trust them?*

#### `examcraft` — *AI Exam Preparation Platform*
Quizzes, flashcards, mock exams, and learning analytics — an end-to-end AI product for learners. The builder side of what I do.

#### `bangla-textbook-retrieval` — *Educational QA Benchmark*
Hierarchy-aware retrieval, dense search, and hybrid fusion for educational question answering in a low-resource language. Grounded AI for learning where it matters most.
